# New Space and IRIS² Educational Context: gnss-orbital-py

This document outlines the professional and political context of the "New Space" ecosystem, detailing how the `gnss-orbital-py` package prepares students for industry roles.

---

## 1. The New Space Ecosystem and the IRIS² Constellation

The global space sector has transitioned from government-dominated, high-cost paradigms to the "New Space" paradigm. New Space is characterized by rapid iteration, low-cost commercial launches, small satellite constellations, and integrated software platforms.

### IRIS²: Europe's Sovereign Constellation
IRIS² (Infrastructure for Resilience, Interconnection and Security by Satellite) is the European Union's multi-orbital satellite constellation. It is designed to provide secure, high-speed connectivity for governmental and commercial applications.

- **Multi-Orbital Architecture**: IRIS² combines LEO (Low Earth Orbit) and MEO (Medium Earth Orbit) nodes to optimize global coverage and minimize signal latency.
- **Strategic Autonomy**: Ensures sovereign European satellite communications, reducing reliance on non-EU commercial constellations.
- **Software Challenges**: Deploying and operating hybrid constellations requires software architectures for orbit propagation, coverage analysis, link budget calculations, and collision avoidance.

## 2. Engineering Competency Framework (CDIO)

The `gnss-orbital-py` project is designed around the CDIO (Conceive, Design, Implement, Operate) syllabus, providing practical experience in satellite software engineering:

- **Conceive**: Define and validate Keplerian orbital elements (semi-major axis, eccentricity, inclination, RAAN, argument of periapsis, true anomaly) for specific missions (LEO, MEO, GEO, HEO).
- **Design**: Apply software engineering patterns (Strategy, Template Method, Observer) to structure orbital calculations.
- **Implement**: Write modular Python code, verify mathematical accuracy with unit tests, and translate interface terms for international operations.
- **Operate**: Model satellite constellations (such as IRIS²) in 3D, analyzing coverage parameters, orbital speeds, and signal latencies.

## 3. Skills for the Modern Aerospace Engineer

Modern space engineering requires combining physics with software design. The largest technical workforce in contemporary space agencies and private aerospace companies consists of software developers.

By working through the notebooks and examples in this repository, students build:
1. **Mathematical Foundations**: Numerical methods for Solving transcendental equations (Kepler's Equation).
2. **Astrodynamics Modeling**: Mapping ECI (Earth-Centered Inertial) coordinates from classical orbital parameters.
3. **Software Architecture**: Writing clean, documentable, internationalized, and modular codebases.
4. **Interactive Data Analysis**: Creating 3D visual models to communicate scientific trajectories.
